HOW IT
WORKS

From clicking the icon to getting your verdict — here's exactly what happens inside TrustCheck, step by step.

// step 01
YOU OPEN A PRODUCT PAGE
Navigate to any product listing on Amazon.in, Flipkart, or Meesho. TrustCheck's content script loads silently in the background, ready to read the page DOM at any moment.
content.js injectedDOM ready
↓
// step 02
YOU CLICK THE TRUSTCHECK ICON
Clicking the 🔍 icon in your Chrome toolbar opens the TrustCheck popup. The popup immediately sends a message to the content script requesting all available product data from the current page.
popup.js opensmessage → content.js
↓
// step 03
CONTENT SCRIPT SCRAPES THE PAGE
The content script reads the live page DOM using platform-specific CSS selectors. It extracts the product name, overall star rating, review titles, and up to 20 individual review bodies. Platform is auto-detected from the URL.
product name star rating review titles up to 20 review bodies platform auto-detected
↓
// step 04
DATA ROUTED THROUGH BACKGROUND WORKER
Scraped data passes from popup.js to background.js — Chrome's Manifest V3 service worker. This is necessary because browser popups cannot make direct cross-origin API calls. The background worker handles all external communication.
background.js handles CORSManifest V3 service worker
↓
// step 05
AI ANALYSES ALL REVIEWS
The background worker sends the product name, platform, star rating, and all review text to the AI API. The model reads every review for authenticity signals, sentiment quality, red flags, fake patterns, and genuine buyer concerns — returning a structured JSON verdict in under 5 seconds.
review authenticity sentiment quality fake signals genuine concerns trust score 0–100
↓
// step 06
VERDICT DISPLAYED IN POPUP
Results are sent back to popup.js and rendered instantly. You see the Trust Score, pros, cons, fake review risk badge, and one clear buying recommendation. Every analysis ends in one of three verdicts:
✅ BUY
Trust Score ≥ 65 and fake risk is not HIGH. Reviews look genuine and specific. Safe to purchase.
⚠️ CAUTION
Score between 39–64. Mixed signals detected. Research further before ordering.
🚫 AVOID
Score ≤ 38 or HIGH fake risk with heavy negative feedback. Strong signs of manipulation.
// what it analyses

8 PARAMETERS
CONSIDERED

TrustCheck doesn't just count stars. It examines 8 distinct dimensions of every review set to build its verdict.

⭐
STAR RATING
The overall numeric rating scraped directly from the product page. Used as a baseline modifier — boosts high-rated products, penalises low-rated ones.
≥ 4.5 → +15 pts≥ 4.0 → +8 pts≤ 3.0 → −15 pts
😊
SENTIMENT RATIO
Each review is scored against curated wordlists of 30+ positive and 30+ negative signals. The ratio of positive to negative reviews forms the largest component of the trust score.
30+ positive words30+ negative words50% of score weight
🤖
FAKE PHRASE DENSITY
Checks how many reviews contain templated fake-review phrases — "highly recommend", "best product ever", "totally worth", "as described" — common in paid review farms.
22+ phrase triggersweighted ×2235% of score weight
📏
REVIEW LENGTH
Very short reviews (under 8 words) are a red flag — genuine buyers usually explain their experience. A high ratio of short reviews significantly lowers the authenticity score.
< 8 words = shortshort ratio × 20catches lazy fakes
🔁
DUPLICATE DETECTION
Every pair of reviews is compared using Jaccard Similarity — a mathematical measure of word overlap. Reviews with over 65% word overlap are flagged as near-duplicates, a strong bot signal.
Jaccard similarity> 65% = duplicateweighted ×40
💬
VAGUENESS SCORE
Reviews that are positive, short, and contain only generic praise with zero product-specific detail are flagged as "vague". High vagueness ratios indicate incentivised or paid reviews.
generic + short + positivevague ratio × 20catches soft fakes
❗
EXCLAMATION DENSITY
Fake reviews frequently overuse exclamation marks to perform enthusiasm. The average exclamation count per review contributes to the fake score, capped at 15 points.
avg per review × 5capped at 15 ptsover-enthusiasm flag
🎯
SPECIFIC CONCERN EXTRACTION
11 concern categories are scanned via regex — durability failures, fake product claims, size mismatch, delivery damage, battery issues, misleading descriptions, and more.
11 concern patternsregex matchedsurfaced as cons

THE THREE
VERDICTS

Every analysis ends with one of three clear verdicts, calculated from the Trust Score and fake review risk level.

✅
BUY
// Trust Score ≥ 65
Reviews look genuine and specific. Positive sentiment dominates without suspicious patterns. Fake review risk is LOW or MEDIUM. Safe to purchase — check the listed cons for dealbreakers.
⚠️
CAUTION
// Trust Score 39–64
Mixed signals detected. Some reviews appear genuine, others are vague or templated. Read the 1-star reviews carefully and compare with alternatives before deciding.
🚫
AVOID
// Trust Score ≤ 38
Too many red flags — high fake review density, heavy complaints, or suspicious review uniformity. The risk of a bad purchase is too high. Look for alternatives.
// trust score formula
50%
Sentiment Ratio
Positive review % minus weighted negative review % across all scraped reviews
35%
Authenticity Score
100 minus fake score — penalises duplicates, generic phrases, short/vague reviews, exclamation overuse
15%
Star Rating Modifier
Scraped overall star rating applies a bonus or penalty to the final score
Score clamped 5–95 · Review count < 5 → −5 penalty · Count ≥ 10 → +5 bonus

WHAT WE

BOT PATTERNS
Identifies repetitive phrasing, templated language, and unnatural positivity used in paid fake review farms across all three platforms.

SENTIMENT DRIFT

INCENTIVE SIGNALS

GENUINE CONCERNS
